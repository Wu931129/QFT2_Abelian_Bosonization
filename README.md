# Abelian Bosonization in 1+1 Dimensions

This repository contains a comprehensive term paper and an accompanying presentation exploring the non-perturbative framework of Abelian bosonization in 1+1 dimensions. These materials were researched, written, and presented as part of the **Quantum Field Theory II** course. 

The project details the exact operator identity between Dirac fermions and scalar bosons, driven by the unique topology of the one-dimensional Fermi surface.

## 🧠 Key Physics Concepts Covered

* **Breakdown of Fermi Liquid Theory:** Analysis of how the collapse of the 1D Fermi surface allows particle-hole pairs to propagate coherently, invalidating Landau's Fermi Liquid theory and giving rise to the Tomonaga-Luttinger Liquid. 
* **The Bosonization Dictionary:** Rigorous mapping of chiral fermionic currents to bosonic gradients, including the necessary introduction of Klein factors to preserve canonical Fermi statistics.
* **Fermions as Topological Solitons:** The physical interpretation of fundamental fermions as topological defects (kinks) within the macroscopic compactified bosonic field. 
* **Sine-Gordon & Massive Thirring Duality:** Examination of how chirality-mixing interactions (such as backscattering and Umklapp processes) map to non-linear cosine potentials, establishing the exact duality between the Massive Thirring model and the Sine-Gordon model.
* **Application to the XXZ Spin Chain:** Utilizing the Jordan-Wigner transformation to map spin-1/2 degrees of freedom to fermions. The bosonization formalism is then applied to determine the phase diagram, identifying the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) transition that separates the gapless XY phase from the gapped Néel phase.
