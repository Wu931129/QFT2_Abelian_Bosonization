# Abelian Bosonization in 1+1 Dimensions

[cite_start]This repository contains a comprehensive term paper and an accompanying presentation exploring the non-perturbative framework of Abelian bosonization in $1+1$ dimensions[cite: 219, 222]. [cite_start]Authored by Yun-Yen Wu [cite: 3, 220][cite_start], these materials detail the exact operator identity between Dirac fermions and scalar bosons, driven by the unique topology of the one-dimensional Fermi surface[cite: 223].

## 🧠 Key Physics Concepts Covered

* [cite_start]**Breakdown of Fermi Liquid Theory:** Analysis of how the collapse of the 1D Fermi surface allows particle-hole pairs to propagate coherently, invalidating Landau's Fermi Liquid theory and giving rise to the Tomonaga-Luttinger Liquid [cite: 37-41, 235-242]. 
* [cite_start]**The Bosonization Dictionary:** Rigorous mapping of chiral fermionic currents to bosonic gradients $(J \sim \partial\varphi)$, including the necessary introduction of Klein factors to preserve canonical Fermi statistics [cite: 205, 224, 406-408].
* [cite_start]**Fermions as Topological Solitons:** The physical interpretation of fundamental fermions as topological defects (kinks) within the macroscopic compactified bosonic field $(\psi \sim e^{i\phi})$ [cite: 206, 438, 444-445]. 
* [cite_start]**Sine-Gordon & Massive Thirring Duality:** Examination of how chirality-mixing interactions (such as backscattering and Umklapp processes) map to non-linear cosine potentials, establishing the exact duality between the Massive Thirring model and the Sine-Gordon model [cite: 226, 597-600].
* [cite_start]**Application to the XXZ Spin Chain:** Utilizing the Jordan-Wigner transformation to map spin-$1/2$ degrees of freedom to fermions [cite: 180-184, 639-640]. [cite_start]The bosonization formalism is then applied to determine the phase diagram, identifying the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless (BKT) transition that separates the gapless XY phase from the gapped Néel phase [cite: 227, 628, 658-663].
